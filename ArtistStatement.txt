










The lifecycle of an idea is fragile.

It begins as a delicate, barely coherent notion: rarely spoken; quickly revised; easily neglected. 

These unstable but hopeful theories can be confronted by an enviornment polluted with toxic cynicism.

My work critically examines the relationships between these unsure thoughts, the vocational obligation intellectual to cultivate, and the unforgiving context into which they are often cast.
